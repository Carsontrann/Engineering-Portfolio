Bolt Cutter Mechanical Analysis

Mechanical Engineering Project | SolidWorks + Static Analysis

Abstract

This lab focused on analyzing the mechanics of a bolt cutter and determining how it produces a large cutting force from a relatively small human applied input. The shear force at the blade was calculated using static equilibrium and found to be 16,696.7 N, approximately 67 times the input force of 250 N.

Introduction

The goal of this lab is to understand how a bolt cutter amplifies human force using mechanical advantage. Bolt cutters use long handles and lever systems to multiply input force, allowing users to cut through strong materials.

How It Works

A bolt cutter uses long handles, pivot joints, and cutting jaws to amplify force. When force is applied at the handles, torque is created about the pivot, which is transferred and amplified through linkages to the cutting jaws.

Conclusion

This lab demonstrated how a relatively small human input force can be amplified into a very large cutting force due to mechanical advantage. The bolt cutter system successfully converts 250 N input force into 16,696.7 N at the blade, showing the effectiveness of lever-based mechanical design.
